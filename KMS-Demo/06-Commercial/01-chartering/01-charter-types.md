# Charter Types

## Purpose

This document describes the different types of charter arrangements.

---

## Contract of Affreightment (COA)

### Definition

Agreement to transport a total quantity over a period, with the shipowner providing suitable vessels.

### Characteristics

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Duration** | Months to years |
| **Volume** | Total quantity agreed |
| **Rate** | Per tonne |
| **Vessel nomination** | Shipowner's choice |
| **Risk** | Shipowner provides capacity |

### When Used

- Long-term customer relationships
- Predictable cargo volumes
- Coal mines with steady production

### Advantages

| For Shipowner | For Charterer |
|---------------|---------------|
| Revenue visibility | Capacity assurance |
| Asset planning | Rate stability |
| Relationship building | Reduced admin |

---

## Time Charter

### Definition

Vessel hired for a period; charterer directs employment, owner provides crewed vessel.

### Characteristics

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Duration** | Weeks to years |
| **Rate** | Daily hire rate |
| **Owner pays** | Crew, insurance, maintenance |
| **Charterer pays** | Fuel, port costs, cargo |

### When Used

- Variable voyage patterns
- Customer needs operational control
- Projects with defined duration

---

## Voyage Charter

### Definition

Single voyage or series of consecutive voyages.

### Characteristics

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Duration** | Single voyage |
| **Rate** | Per tonne or lump sum |
| **Owner pays** | All operating costs |
| **Charterer pays** | Loading/discharge |

### When Used

- Spot cargo opportunities
- One-off shipments
- Market rate exposure wanted

---

## Comparison

| Feature | COA | Time Charter | Voyage |
|---------|-----|--------------|--------|
| Duration | Long | Medium | Single |
| Rate type | $/tonne | $/day | $/tonne |
| Fuel cost | Owner | Charterer | Owner |
| Port costs | Owner | Charterer | Owner |
| Flexibility | Medium | High | Low |
