# Permit to Work System

## Purpose

The Permit to Work (PTW) system is a formal documented system used to control certain types of work that are identified as potentially hazardous. A permit ensures that all foreseeable hazards have been identified and that appropriate precautions are in place.

---

## Scope

Applies to all high-risk activities on:
- Floating Loading Facilities (FLF)
- Tugs and Barges
- Motor Vessels
- Shore-based facilities

---

## When is a Permit Required?

| Activity | Permit Type | Approver |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Welding, cutting, grinding | Hot Work Permit | Master / Chief Officer |
| Entry into tanks, cargo holds, void spaces | Confined Space Entry Permit | Master / Chief Officer |
| Work above 2 meters with risk of fall | Work at Heights Permit | Master / Chief Officer |
| Electrical work on live systems | Electrical Isolation Permit | Chief Engineer |
| Work on pressurized systems | Pressure System Permit | Chief Engineer |
| Diving operations | Diving Permit | DPA / Operations Manager |
| Crane/lifting operations (critical lifts) | Lifting Permit | Master / Chief Officer |

## General Permit Requirements

### Before Work Starts:
- [ ] Job Safety Analysis (JSA) completed
- [ ] Hazards identified and controls in place
- [ ] Personnel briefed on scope, hazards, and emergency procedures
- [ ] Required PPE available and worn
- [ ] Communication established (radio check)
- [ ] Standby person assigned (where required)
- [ ] Tools and equipment inspected
- [ ] Permit signed by Authorizing Officer

### During Work:
- [ ] Permit displayed at work location
- [ ] Conditions continuously monitored
- [ ] Stop work if conditions change
- [ ] Re-assess if scope changes

### After Work:
- [ ] Area restored to safe condition
- [ ] Equipment secured
- [ ] Permit closed out and signed off
- [ ] Retained for records (minimum 3 months)

---

## Hot Work Permit

### Definition
Any work involving open flames, sparks, or high heat (welding, cutting, grinding, burning).

### Additional Requirements
| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Gas-free certificate** | If in or near enclosed spaces/tanks |
| **Fire watch** | During work and 30 min after |
| **Fire extinguisher** | At work location |
| **Combustibles removed** | 11-meter radius cleared |
| **Notification** | Bridge and engine room informed |

### Hot Work Not Permitted When:
- Cargo operations ongoing (coal, flammable cargo)
- Gas-dangerous atmosphere detected
- Adjacent spaces not gas-free
- Fire detection system isolated without approval

---

## Confined Space Entry Permit

### Definition
Any enclosed or partially enclosed space not designed for continuous human occupancy with restricted entry/exit (tanks, cargo holds, void spaces, ballast tanks).

### Additional Requirements
| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Atmosphere testing** | O2: 19.5-23.5%, LEL: <1%, H2S: <10ppm, CO: <25ppm |
| **Continuous ventilation** | Before and during entry |
| **Standby person** | At entry point at all times |
| **Rescue equipment** | Ready for immediate use |
| **Communication** | Continuous contact with standby |
| **Entry log** | Names and times recorded |

### Entry Not Permitted When:
- Atmosphere test fails
- No standby person available
- Rescue equipment not ready
- Ventilation cannot be maintained

---

## Work at Heights Permit

### Definition
Any work where a person could fall 2 meters or more.

### Additional Requirements
| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Fall protection** | Harness with two lanyards attached |
| **Anchor points** | Certified and inspected |
| **Exclusion zone** | Below work area barricaded |
| **Tools secured** | Lanyards on tools to prevent drops |
| **Weather check** | Not in high wind, rain, or heavy swell |

---

## Permit Validity

| Permit Type | Maximum Duration | Extension |
|-------------|------------------|-----------|
| Hot Work | 8 hours (one shift) | New permit required |
| Confined Space | 8 hours (one shift) | Re-test atmosphere |
| Work at Heights | 8 hours (one shift) | Re-assess conditions |
| Electrical Isolation | Until work complete | Must not exceed 24h |

**Note:** A permit is automatically suspended if:
- Emergency alarm sounds
- Conditions change significantly
- Personnel leave the work area

---

## Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| **Person in Charge (PIC)** | Request permit, complete JSA, brief crew, ensure compliance |
| **Authorizing Officer** | Verify precautions, issue permit, conduct checks |
| **Standby Person** | Monitor workers, maintain communication, initiate rescue |
| **Workers** | Follow permit conditions, stop if unsafe, report issues |

---

## Records

All completed permits shall be:
- Retained on board for minimum 3 months
- Available for audit review
- Logged in permit register
