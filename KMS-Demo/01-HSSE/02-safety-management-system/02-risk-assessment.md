# Risk Assessment Procedures

## Purpose

This document establishes the procedures for identifying hazards, assessing risks, and implementing controls to ensure safe operations across all IMC Pelita Logistik vessels and facilities.

---

## Scope

Applies to:
- All vessel types (FLF, Tug & Barge, Motor Vessels)
- All operational activities
- All personnel (employees and contractors)

---

## 1. Hazard Identification (HAZID)

### When Required
- New vessel acquisition
- New operation routes
- Change in cargo type
- Major modification to equipment
- After serious incident

### Process
1. Assemble multi-disciplinary team (Operations, HSSE, Crew)
2. Review activity step-by-step
3. Identify potential hazards at each step
4. Assess likelihood and consequence
5. Determine existing controls
6. Identify additional controls needed
7. Document and communicate findings

---

## 2. Job Safety Analysis (JSA)

### When Required
- Before non-routine tasks
- High-risk activities (heights, confined space, hot work)
- When conditions change from normal
- New crew unfamiliar with task

### JSA Form Sections

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Task Description** | What work is being done |
| **Location** | Where (vessel, deck, engine room, etc.) |
| **Crew Involved** | Names and roles |
| **Steps** | Break down task into sequential steps |
| **Hazards** | What could go wrong at each step |
| **Controls** | How hazards are controlled |
| **PPE Required** | Specific PPE for this task |
| **Permits Required** | Hot work, confined space, work at heights |
| **Emergency Plan** | What to do if something goes wrong |

### Approval
- JSA reviewed and signed by Supervisor/Officer
- Crew briefed on JSA before work starts
- JSA kept at work site during task

---

## 3. Take 5 / Last Minute Risk Assessment (LMRA)

Before starting **any** task, every worker should spend 30 seconds to "Take 5":

| Step | Question |
|------|----------|
| **1. STOP** | Do I know what I'm doing? |
| **2. THINK** | What could go wrong? |
| **3. ASSESS** | Do I have the right tools, PPE, and permits? |
| **4. CONTROL** | What can I do to make it safer? |
| **5. PROCEED** | Is it safe to start? If not, STOP and report. |

## Risk Matrix

### Likelihood Scale

| Rating | Likelihood | Description |
|--------|------------|-------------|
| 1 | Rare | Could occur in exceptional circumstances |
| 2 | Unlikely | Could occur at some time |
| 3 | Possible | Might occur at some time |
| 4 | Likely | Will probably occur in most circumstances |
| 5 | Almost Certain | Expected to occur in most circumstances |

### Consequence Scale

| Rating | Consequence | People | Environment | Asset |
|--------|-------------|--------|-------------|-------|
| 1 | Insignificant | First aid | No impact | < $10K |
| 2 | Minor | Medical treatment | Minor spill | $10K-$100K |
| 3 | Moderate | Lost time injury | Localized impact | $100K-$1M |
| 4 | Major | Permanent disability | Significant impact | $1M-$10M |
| 5 | Catastrophic | Fatality | Major environmental | > $10M |

### Risk Matrix

|   | **1-Rare** | **2-Unlikely** | **3-Possible** | **4-Likely** | **5-Certain** |
|---|------------|----------------|----------------|--------------|---------------|
| **5-Catastrophic** | High | High | Extreme | Extreme | Extreme |
| **4-Major** | Medium | High | High | Extreme | Extreme |
| **3-Moderate** | Low | Medium | High | High | Extreme |
| **2-Minor** | Low | Low | Medium | Medium | High |
| **1-Insignificant** | Low | Low | Low | Medium | Medium |

### Risk Response

| Risk Level | Action Required |
|------------|-----------------|
| **Extreme** | Stop work. Implement additional controls before proceeding. DPA notification required. |
| **High** | Senior officer approval required. Additional controls must be in place. |
| **Medium** | Supervisor approval. Ensure controls are effective. |
| **Low** | Proceed with standard controls. Monitor. |

---

## Hierarchy of Controls

When selecting controls, apply in this order (most to least effective):

1. **Elimination** – Remove the hazard completely
2. **Substitution** – Replace with something less hazardous
3. **Engineering Controls** – Isolate people from the hazard
4. **Administrative Controls** – Change the way people work
5. **PPE** – Personal Protective Equipment (last resort)
