# HSSE Policy & Leadership Commitment

## Management Commitment Statement

PT IMC Pelita Logistik is committed to achieving the highest standards of Health, Safety, Security, and Environmental (HSSE) performance across all our operations. As a leading maritime logistics provider in Indonesia, we recognize that our success depends on the safety and well-being of our people and the protection of the environment we operate in.

**We commit to:**

1. **Preventing harm** to our employees, contractors, customers, and communities
2. **Protecting the environment** from pollution and minimizing our environmental footprint
3. **Complying with all applicable** laws, regulations, and international maritime conventions
4. **Continuously improving** our HSSE performance through learning and innovation
5. **Empowering all personnel** to stop work if conditions are unsafe

> *"Safety is not a priority that can be changed – it is a value that guides everything we do."*
> 
> — **Board of Directors, PT IMC Pelita Logistik Tbk**

## HSSE Objectives

### Annual Objectives (2026)

| Objective | Target | Measurement |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **Zero Fatalities** | 0 | Year-end count |
| **Lost Time Injury Rate (LTIR)** | < 0.5 | Per million man-hours |
| **Oil Spills to Sea** | 0 | Year-end count |
| **Port State Control Detentions** | 0 | Year-end count |
| **Near Miss Reporting** | > 100 reports | Proactive safety culture indicator |
| **HSSE Training Completion** | 100% | All mandatory training completed |
| **Drill Completion Rate** | 100% | All scheduled drills conducted |

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

KPIs are reviewed monthly by the HSSE Committee and reported to the Board quarterly.

## Roles & Responsibilities

### Board of Directors
- Set HSSE policy and strategic direction
- Allocate resources for HSSE management
- Review HSSE performance quarterly

### President Director (CEO)
- Overall accountability for HSSE performance
- Approve major HSSE initiatives and investments

### Director of Operations
- Ensure operational activities comply with HSSE requirements
- Investigate and address systemic safety issues

### Designated Person Ashore (DPA)
- Direct link between vessels and shore management for safety
- Ensure ISM Code compliance
- Authority to mobilize resources in emergencies

### HSSE Manager
- Develop and maintain HSSE management system
- Coordinate audits, inspections, and training
- Analyze incidents and drive corrective actions

### Fleet Superintendent
- Ensure vessels are maintained to safe standards
- Support vessel Masters with safety issues

### Vessel Master/Captain
- Full authority and responsibility for safety on board
- **Right and duty to stop operations** if safety is at risk
- Conduct drills and maintain crew competency

### All Employees & Crew
- Follow HSSE procedures and use required PPE
- Report hazards, near misses, and incidents
- **Stop work if conditions are unsafe**

---

## Stop Work Authority

**Every employee has the authority and obligation to stop work** when:

- Conditions are unsafe
- Procedures are not being followed
- Required permits are not in place
- PPE is not available or not being used
- Weather or sea conditions exceed safe limits

> **No one will be disciplined for stopping work due to safety concerns.**
