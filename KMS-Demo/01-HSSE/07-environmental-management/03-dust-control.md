# Dust Control

## Purpose

This document provides procedures for controlling coal dust during transshipment and cargo operations to protect personnel and the environment.

---

## Scope

Applies to: **FLF, Motor Vessels, and Barges handling coal cargo**

---

## Coal Dust Hazards

| Hazard | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Respiratory illness** | Inhalation causes lung damage |
| **Explosion** | Airborne dust is explosive |
| **Slip hazard** | Deck accumulation is slippery |
| **Environmental** | Dust contaminates marine environment |
| **Visibility** | Heavy dust reduces visibility |
| **Equipment damage** | Dust infiltrates machinery |

---

## Dust Generation Sources

| Source | Operation |
|--------|-----------|
| Grab discharge | Opening grab over hoppers |
| Conveyor transfer points | Belt-to-belt transfer |
| Loading chutes | Dropping cargo into holds |
| Sweeping/cleaning | Deck cleaning operations |
| High wind | Blowing across exposed cargo |

---

## Dust Suppression Methods

### Water Spray Systems

| Method | Application |
|--------|-------------|
| **Fixed spray bars** | At conveyor transfer points |
| **Mobile spray** | Handheld hoses for spot control |
| **Fog/mist systems** | Fine mist suppresses airborne dust |

> **Note:** Avoid over-wetting cargo – excess moisture affects cargo quality and increases liquefaction risk.

### Operational Controls

| Control | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Reduce drop height** | Lower chutes/conveyors to minimize fall |
| **Wind awareness** | Position operations to minimize wind effect |
| **Speed control** | Reduce conveyor/grab speed if dust excessive |
| **Enclosures** | Cover transfer points where possible |

---

## During Loading Operations

| Requirement | Action |
|-------------|--------|
| Monitor wind direction | Adjust position if possible |
| Activate spray systems | Before starting operations |
| Reduce drop height | As low as practical |
| Stop if excessive | If dust is uncontrollable |
| PPE for exposed crew | Dust masks mandatory |

---

## During Discharge Operations

| Requirement | Action |
|-------------|--------|
| Grab opening | Open slowly over hopper |
| Water spray | Apply at hopper area |
| Deck washing | Regular wash down during ops |
| Visibility | Stop if visibility dangerously reduced |

---

## PPE for Dust Exposure

| PPE | When Required |
|-----|---------------|
| **Dust mask (P2/N95)** | All cargo operations with visible dust |
| **Safety glasses** | Cargo operations |
| **Coveralls** | Full coverage to prevent skin irritation |
| **Gloves** | Handling dusty materials |

---

## Deck Cleaning

### During Operations

| Requirement | Frequency |
|-------------|-----------|
| Deck wash-down | Every shift during ops |
| Accumulation removal | Before becoming slip hazard |
| Drainage | Ensure scuppers flowing (not directly to sea with heavy load) |

### After Operations

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Complete wash-down of all affected areas |
| 2 | Clean equipment of dust accumulation |
| 3 | Check machinery spaces for dust ingress |
| 4 | Document any equipment concerns |

---

## Environmental Considerations

### Preventing Dust to Sea

| Measure | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Water spray | Settle dust on deck, not overboard |
| Screen doors | Contain dust spray drift |
| Washdown collection | Allow settling before discharge |

### Client Requirements

Some clients (e.g., Adaro) have **zero contamination** policies:
- Cargo must not contaminate sea
- Strict dust control required
- May require reporting of any incidents

---

## Stopping Operations

Stop cargo operations if:
- ⚠️ Visibility reduced to unsafe level
- ⚠️ Unable to control dust adequately
- ⚠️ PPE unavailable
- ⚠️ Client requirements cannot be met
- ⚠️ Wind conditions exceed limits
