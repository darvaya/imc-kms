# Incident Management

This section covers the procedures for reporting, classifying, and investigating incidents to learn from them and prevent recurrence.

---

## Why Report Incidents?

- **Prevent recurrence** – Learn from what went wrong
- **Legal compliance** – Required by regulations
- **Continuous improvement** – Drive safety performance
- **Insurance** – Document for claims
- **Crew protection** – Record for medical/compensation

---

## Reporting Culture

We operate a **Just Culture** where:

- ✅ Honest reporting is encouraged
- ✅ Near misses are valued as learning opportunities
- ✅ Human error is generally treated as a system failure
- ❌ Deliberate violations are not tolerated
- ❌ Gross negligence has consequences

> **No retaliation** for good-faith incident reporting.

## Incident Types

| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| **Near Miss** | Almost happened but no harm |
| **First Aid Case** | Minor treatment on board |
| **Medical Treatment Case** | Professional medical care |
| **Lost Time Injury (LTI)** | Unable to work next shift |
| **Fatality** | Death |
| **Property Damage** | Vessel, cargo, equipment |
| **Environmental** | Spill, pollution |
| **Security** | Theft, piracy, stowaway |

---

## Reporting Timelines

| Incident Severity | Initial Report | Full Report |
|-------------------|----------------|-------------|
| Fatality / Major Injury | Immediately | Within 24 hours |
| LTI | Within 4 hours | Within 48 hours |
| Medical Treatment | Within 8 hours | Within 72 hours |
| Property/Environmental | Within 4 hours | Within 72 hours |
| Near Miss | Within 24 hours | Within 7 days |
