# Incident Classification

## Purpose

This document provides the classification system for incidents to ensure consistent categorization and appropriate response.

---

## Scope

Applies to: **All incidents on all vessels and facilities**

---

## Personal Injury Classification

| Classification | Definition | Reporting |
|----------------|------------|-----------|
| **Near Miss** | Incident that could have caused injury but did not | Internal |
| **First Aid Case (FAC)** | Minor injury treated on board, no medical professional needed | Internal |
| **Medical Treatment Case (MTC)** | Injury requiring professional medical treatment | DPA + Flag |
| **Restricted Work Case (RWC)** | Injured but can do limited duties | DPA + Flag |
| **Lost Time Injury (LTI)** | Unable to work next scheduled shift | DPA + Flag + Investigation |
| **Permanent Disability** | Permanent loss of function | DPA + Flag + Full Investigation |
| **Fatality** | Death from work-related injury | Immediate + All authorities |

---

## What Counts as LTI?

### LTI = Lost Time Injury

An LTI occurs when an injured person is **unable to work their next scheduled shift** due to a work-related injury.

| Scenario | Classification |
|----------|----------------|
| Injured at 0900, returns to light duties same day | RWC |
| Injured at 0900, cannot work night shift | LTI |
| Injured at 0900, off work for 3 days | LTI |
| Injury during off-duty hours, work-related | LTI (if meets criteria) |

---

## Lost Time Injury Frequency Rate (LTIFR)

**LTIFR** = (Number of LTIs × 1,000,000) ÷ Total Man-Hours Worked

| Example | |
|---------|--|
| LTIs in period | 2 |
| Man-hours worked | 2,000,000 |
| LTIFR | 1.0 |

### Benchmarks

| Industry | Typical LTIFR |
|----------|---------------|
| Best-in-class maritime | <0.5 |
| Average maritime | 1.0-2.0 |
| Our target | <0.5 |

## Environmental Incident Classification

| Tier | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Tier 1** | Minor, contained on board | Small deck spill, cleaned up |
| **Tier 2** | Moderate, limited external | Oil sheen visible in water, minor |
| **Tier 3** | Major, significant external | Large spill, shoreline impact |

---

## Property/Asset Damage Classification

| Level | Description | Cost |
|-------|-------------|------|
| **Minor** | No impact on operations | <$10,000 |
| **Moderate** | Short-term impact | $10,000-$100,000 |
| **Major** | Significant impact | $100,000-$1,000,000 |
| **Critical** | Loss of asset or extended downtime | >$1,000,000 |

## Near Miss Importance

> **"Every accident was once a near miss"**

Near misses are **leading indicators** of safety performance. Reporting near misses allows us to:

- Fix hazards before they cause injury
- Identify trends and patterns
- Celebrate proactive safety behavior

We **encourage** near miss reporting with a target of >100 reports per year.

---

## High Potential Incident (HiPo)

A **High Potential Incident** is any incident that *could have* resulted in:
- Fatality
- Permanent disability
- Major environmental impact

Even if actual consequences were minor, HiPos require **full investigation**.

### Examples:
- Near miss with dropped load from crane
- Fire quickly extinguished
- Person fell but was caught by harness
- Oil spill that was contained on deck
