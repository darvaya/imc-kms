# Cargo Hold Fire (Coal)

## Purpose

This document provides specialized procedures for responding to cargo hold fires on Motor Vessels (MV) carrying coal. Coal fires require different handling than other fires due to their self-heating properties.

---

## Scope

Applies to: **Motor Vessels (Bulk Carriers) carrying coal cargo**

---

## Coal Fire Hazards

| Hazard | Risk |
|--------|------|
| **Self-heating** | Coal can spontaneously combust at temperatures above 40-60°C |
| **Methane release** | Coal releases flammable gas that can create explosive atmosphere |
| **Oxygen depletion** | Coal absorbs oxygen – holds may become oxygen-deficient |
| **Re-ignition** | Coal fires can smolder and reignite even after apparent extinguishment |
| **Water reaction** | Water on hot coal creates steam and may cause coal dust explosion |

---

## Coal Fire Warning Signs

| Indicator | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Rising cargo temperature (>55°C) | Increase monitoring, consider ventilation |
| Smoke or unusual smell from holds | Investigate immediately |
| Gas detector alarm (methane/CO) | Close hold, seal ventilation |
| Visible flames | Emergency procedures |

---

## Immediate Response

### Upon Confirming Cargo Hold Fire

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **1. SOUND ALARM** | General emergency alarm |
| **2. STOP VENTILATION** | Close all ventilation to affected hold |
| **3. SEAL THE HOLD** | Ensure all hatch covers, vents, manholes closed |
| **4. DO NOT OPEN HATCH** | Opening provides oxygen, intensifies fire |
| **5. COOL BOUNDARIES** | Apply water to deck and bulkheads to prevent spread |
| **6. NOTIFY** | Bridge: "Fire in cargo hold [number]" |

---

## Firefighting Strategy

### Primary Method: Sealing and Smothering

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Ensure complete sealing of cargo hold |
| 2 | Inject CO2 into hold (if fixed system available) |
| 3 | Alternatively, inject inert gas or steam |
| 4 | Maintain boundary cooling |
| 5 | Monitor temperature continuously |
| 6 | Do not break seal for at least 24-48 hours |

### CO2 Flooding

| Consideration | Details |
|---------------|---------|
| **Calculation** | Ensure sufficient CO2 for hold volume |
| **Holding time** | CO2 must be maintained – no leaks |
| **Re-ignition risk** | Fire may reignite when hold opened |
| **Personnel safety** | No entry until gas-free confirmed |

---

## Water – Use With Caution

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Water should be used with extreme caution on coal fires.

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Steam explosion | Use fine spray, not jet |
| Coal dust explosion | Do not disturb coal surface |
| Stability | Monitor bilge levels, free surface effect |
| Cargo damage | Consider insurance/cargo interest |

**When water may be used:**
- If hold cannot be sealed
- Boundary cooling (external surfaces)
- At the advice of salvors or Class

## Communication Checklist

| Contact | When | Information |
|---------|------|-------------|
| **DPA / Company** | Immediately | Fire confirmed, actions taken |
| **Cargo interests** | As soon as practical | Nature of incident |
| **P&I Club** | Within 24 hours | Potential claim |
| **Flag State** | If serious | Reporting requirement |
| **Class Society** | If damage | Survey requirement |
| **Coast Guard** | If assistance needed | Position, status |

---

## Proceeding to Port

If cargo hold fire is confirmed:

| Action | Consideration |
|--------|---------------|
| **Divert to nearest port** | Shortest safe route |
| **Inform port authorities** | Fire on board, potential assistance needed |
| **Request firefighters** | Standby on arrival |
| **Maintain boundary cooling** | Throughout voyage |
| **Calculate stability** | If flooding hold is considered |

---

## Post-Fire Procedures

### Before Opening Hold:

- [ ] Temperature stable/declining for 24+ hours
- [ ] Gas readings acceptable (O2, CO, CH4)
- [ ] Fire team ready with hoses and BA
- [ ] Port fire brigade on standby
- [ ] Slow, controlled opening only

### After Opening:

| Action | Responsibility |
|--------|----------------|
| Continue monitoring | Chief Officer |
| Do not disturb cargo until cool | All |
| Refer to cargointerests | Master |
| Class survey required | Master |
| Full investigation | HSSE Manager |

---

## Prevention: Cargo Temperature Monitoring

| Check | Frequency | Action Level |
|-------|-----------|--------------|
| Hold temperature | Every 4 hours | >55°C investigate |
| Methane levels | Daily | >1% LEL – seal and monitor |
| CO levels | Daily | >30 ppm – investigate |
