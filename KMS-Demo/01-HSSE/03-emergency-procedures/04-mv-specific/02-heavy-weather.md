# Heavy Weather Operations

## Purpose

This document provides procedures for safely navigating Motor Vessels through heavy weather conditions, including preparation, operations, and crew safety.

---

## Scope

Applies to: **Motor Vessels (Bulk Carriers) operating on international and coastal voyages**

---

## Heavy Weather Definition

| Condition | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Rough seas** | Significant wave height > 4 meters |
| **Strong winds** | Beaufort 7+ (> 28 knots sustained) |
| **Tropical storm/cyclone** | Organized weather system, winds > 34 knots |
| **Restricted visibility** | Fog, heavy rain < 0.5 nm visibility |

---

## Pre-Voyage Weather Planning

### Weather Assessment

| Action | Responsibility |
|--------|----------------|
| Obtain current synoptic chart | Master / 2/O |
| Check 5-day forecast along route | Master |
| Identify potential weather systems | Master |
| Plan alternate routes if needed | Master |
| Consider port of refuge options | Master |

### Voyage Plan Updates

- Mark expected weather windows on chart
- Identify areas to avoid (shallow water + waves = dangerous)
- Calculate ETA adjustments for reduced speed
- Communicate plan to company

## Heavy Weather Operations

### Speed and Heading

| Condition | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **Head seas** | Reduce speed to prevent slamming and green water |
| **Following seas** | Adjust speed to avoid synchronous rolling |
| **Beam seas** | Alter course to reduce roll amplitude |
| **Quartering seas** | Beware parametric rolling – alter course |

### Speed Guidelines

| Situation | Guidance |
|-----------|----------|
| Excessive pitching | Reduce speed 1-2 knots increments |
| Propeller racing | Reduce speed until eliminated |
| Shipping green water | Reduce speed, alter course |
| Slamming | Reduce speed significantly |

---

## Crew Safety

### Movement on Deck

> ⚠️ **DECK ACCESS RESTRICTED** during heavy weather unless absolutely essential.

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Permission** | Master's approval for any deck access |
| **Buddy system** | Minimum 2 persons |
| **Communication** | Continuous radio contact with bridge |
| **PPE** | Lifejacket, safety harness, safety boots |
| **Lifelines** | Use dedicated lifelines where fitted |
| **Timing** | During relative calm between wave sets |

### Inside Ship

- Use handrails at all times
- Secure loose items in cabins
- Hot liquids in spill-proof containers only
- Gym equipment secured
- Medical kit ready for motion injuries

---

## Cargo Considerations (Coal/Bulk)

| Concern | Action |
|---------|--------|
| **Cargo shift** | Monitor for list; if develops, investigate |
| **Liquefaction** | Monitor if cargo has high moisture content |
| **Hold ventilation** | May need to close if shipping water |
| **Hatch covers** | Confirm fully sealed and secured |

## Communication

| Update | To Whom | Frequency |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Weather conditions | Company / Operations | Every 4-6 hours |
| Position report | Company | Every 6 hours |
| ETA adjustments | Agent/Port | As needed |
| Distress/assistance | Coast Guard | If in danger |

---

## When to Seek Shelter

Consider port of refuge if:
- Crew safety at risk
- Structural damage occurring
- Cargo shift suspected
- Unable to make meaningful headway
- Weather deteriorating beyond forecast

> Master has full authority to deviate for safety.
