# Towing Line Failure

## Purpose

This document provides emergency procedures for towing line (towline) failure during tug and barge operations.

---

## Scope

Applies to: **Tugs and Barges only**

---

## Towline Failure Hazards

| Hazard | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|
| **Snapback** | Extreme force when line parts – fatal injuries |
| **Loss of control** | Barge drifts, collision risk |
| **Capsize** | Sudden load release can destabilize tug |
| **Grounding** | Uncontrolled barge may run aground |

---

## Snapback Zone

> ⚠️ **DANGER:** When under tension, a towline stores enormous energy. If it parts, it will recoil violently.

### Snapback Zone Definition
The area where a broken line could strike is the **snapback zone**. This extends outward in a cone from each attachment point.

**KEEP CLEAR** of:
- Towing bitts and winch areas when line is under load
- Deck areas in line with the towline lead
- Areas behind chocks and fairleads

---

## Prevention Measures

| Measure | Responsibility |
|---------|----------------|
| Regular towline inspection | Chief Officer |
| Replace worn lines before failure | Master |
| Maintain line within safe working load | Master |
| Weather monitoring – reduce load in heavy weather | Master |
| Clear deck of personnel when heaving up | Chief Officer |
| Use pennant wire or spring (shock absorber) | Master |

---

## Immediate Response – Towline Parts

### On the Tug

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **1. SOUND ALARM** | Alert all crew |
| **2. ALL HANDS CLEAR** | Personnel away from deck |
| **3. STOP ENGINES** | Reduce forward motion |
| **4. CHECK INJURIES** | Anyone struck by parted line? |
| **5. ASSESS** | Has tug lost stability? (sudden load release) |
| **6. MONITOR BARGE** | Track barge position, drift direction |
| **7. NOTIFY** | "All stations, [Tug name], towline parted, barge adrift at [position]" |

### On the Barge

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **1. DROP ANCHOR** | If manned and in shallow water |
| **2. SOUND SIGNAL** | If unmanned, tug should monitor |
| **3. MONITOR DRIFT** | Toward hazards? Other vessels? |

---

## Recovery Procedures

### If Conditions Allow Reconnection:

1. Tug maneuvers alongside barge (carefully, maintain safe distance)
2. Crew prepares new towline or messenger line
3. Use heaving line to pass messenger
4. Connect new towline with proper gear
5. Slow take-up of slack, avoid shock loading
6. Resume towing at reduced speed initially

### If Conditions Too Hazardous:

1. Do not attempt reconnection in heavy weather
2. Monitor barge drift, alert other traffic
3. Request assistance (another tug, Coast Guard)
4. Attempt to prevent grounding if barge drifting to shoal

---

## If Crew Member Injured by Snapback

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Do not move casualty unless in immediate danger |
| 2 | Call for medical assistance |
| 3 | Assess injuries – snapback often causes fractures, internal injuries |
| 4 | Administer first aid as trained |
| 5 | Prepare for MEDEVAC – this is likely a serious injury |
| 6 | Preserve the scene for investigation |

## Reporting

| Report | To Whom | When |
|--------|---------|------|
| Verbal notification | Operations / DPA | Immediately |
| Written incident report | HSSE Manager | Within 24 hours |
| Investigation | Master / HSSE | Within 48 hours |
| Line failure analysis | Technical team | As required |

## Post-Incident Actions

| Action | Responsibility |
|--------|----------------|
| Photograph parted line ends | Master |
| Retain failed line section for analysis | Master |
| Record conditions (weather, load, duration) | Master |
| Inspect all other towing equipment | Chief Officer |
| Crew debrief | Master |
| Review procedures | HSSE Manager |
