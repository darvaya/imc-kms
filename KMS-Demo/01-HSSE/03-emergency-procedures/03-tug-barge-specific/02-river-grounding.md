# River Grounding Procedures

## Purpose

This document provides emergency procedures when a tug, barge, or tug-barge combination runs aground in river or shallow water operations.

---

## Scope

Applies to: **Tugs and Barges operating in Indonesian rivers** (Mahakam, Barito, Kapuas, etc.)

---

## River Grounding Factors

| Factor | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Shifting channels** | River channels change with sediment movement |
| **Low water** | Dry season reduces navigable depth |
| **Overloading** | Barge draft exceeds channel depth |
| **Navigation error** | Straying from safe channel |
| **Current** | Set into shoal water |
| **Poor visibility** | Night, fog, rain |

---

## Types of Grounding

| Type | Severity | Description |
|------|----------|-------------|
| **Soft grounding** | Low | Mud/sand bottom, vessel stuck but no damage |
| **Hard grounding** | Medium | Rock/hard bottom, potential hull damage |
| **Stranding** | High | Vessel high and dry, major effort to refloat |

---

## Immediate Response

### Upon Grounding

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **1. STOP ENGINES** | Prevent driving harder aground or propeller damage |
| **2. SOUND ALARM** | Alert all crew |
| **3. CHECK DAMAGE** | Sound all tanks, look for water ingress |
| **4. NOTIFY** | "All stations, [Vessel name] aground at [position]" |
| **5. MARK POSITION** | GPS coordinates, note tide state |
| **6. ASSESS** | Type of bottom, severity, tide rising or falling |

---

## Initial Assessment Checklist

- [ ] All crew accounted for and safe
- [ ] Hull integrity – any breaches or water ingress
- [ ] Engine and propeller status
- [ ] Stability – any listing
- [ ] Surrounding water depth
- [ ] Traffic in area notified
- [ ] Tide state (rising or falling)
- [ ] Weather forecast

---

## If Taking Water

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Activate bilge pumps |
| 2 | Locate ingress point |
| 3 | Attempt temporary repair (wooden plugs, cement box) |
| 4 | Monitor soundings continuously |
| 5 | Transfer cargo/ballast to correct list if safe |
| 6 | Prepare for evacuation if vessel at risk |
| 7 | Request immediate assistance |

---

## Refloating Options

### Self-Refloating (if conditions allow)

| Method | When to Use |
|--------|-------------|
| **Wait for tide** | If tide is rising and no damage |
| **Ballast adjustment** | Shift ballast to change trim/list |
| **Lightering** | Transfer cargo to another barge |
| **Kedging** | Deploy anchor and winch off |
| **Astern power** | If soft grounding, try reversing (carefully) |

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Do not apply excessive engine power – may cause propeller damage or drive further aground.

### External Assistance

| Assistance | When Needed |
|------------|-------------|
| **Second tug** | To provide pulling power |
| **Salvage tug** | If stranded or hard grounding |
| **Floating crane** | To remove cargo for lightening |
| **Dredger** | To clear channel around vessel |

---

## Cargo Considerations (Coal Barge)

| Issue | Action |
|-------|--------|
| **Overloading** | May need to lighter cargo to reduce draft |
| **Stability** | Removing cargo can affect stability – calculate carefully |
| **Environment** | Prevent cargo entering water during lightening |

## Post-Refloating Actions

| Action | Responsibility | Timeline |
|--------|----------------|----------|
| Full hull inspection | Master / Chief Officer | Immediately |
| Tank soundings | Chief Officer | Immediately |
| Sea trial | Master | Before resuming |
| Underwater survey | Diver / Class | If any concerns |
| Incident report | Master | Within 24 hours |
| Investigation | HSSE Manager | Within 48 hours |

---

## Prevention

| Measure | Responsibility |
|---------|----------------|
| Updated river charts | Master |
| Draft surveys before sailing | Chief Officer |
| Local pilot/advice in unfamiliar rivers | Master |
| Speed reduction in shallow areas | Master |
| Depth sounder monitoring | Helmsman |
| Check tide tables | Master |
