# Fire Emergency Procedures

## Purpose

This document provides the procedures for responding to fire emergencies on all IMC Pelita Logistik vessels.

---

## Scope

Applies to: **All vessel types** (FLF, Tug & Barge, Motor Vessels)

---

## Fire Triangle

Fire requires three elements. Remove any one to extinguish:

```
        HEAT
         /\
        /  \
       /    \
      /  🔥  \
     /________\
  FUEL        OXYGEN
```

---

## Immediate Response (First Responder)

### Upon Discovering Fire

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **1. RAISE ALARM** | Shout "FIRE FIRE FIRE!" + location. Activate nearest fire alarm |
| **2. INFORM BRIDGE** | Radio: "Bridge, this is [Name], fire in [Location]" |
| **3. ATTEMPT TO FIGHT** | Only if safe and fire is small. Use appropriate extinguisher |
| **4. ISOLATE** | Close doors/dampers to prevent spread. Do not open hatches |
| **5. EVACUATE** | If fire grows, evacuate the space. Account for personnel |

---

## Fire Alarm Response (All Personnel)

| Location | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **All crew** | Stop work, proceed to muster station, don lifejacket |
| **Bridge** | Sound general alarm, prepare for maneuvering |
| **Engine Room** | Standby for emergency stops, ventilation control |
| **Fire Team** | Muster at fire station, don gear, await orders |
| **Medical Team** | Prepare medical station for casualties |

---

## Fire Team Response

### Fire Team Composition
- **Team Leader** (usually Chief Officer)
- **2 Fire Fighters** (BA wearers)
- **2 Support** (hose handling, backup)
- **1 Boundary Cooling** (external)

### Response Actions

| Phase | Actions |
|-------|---------|
| **Assessment** | Locate fire, assess size, determine class |
| **Boundary Cooling** | Cool adjacent bulkheads to prevent spread |
| **Ventilation** | Secure ventilation to affected space |
| **Attack** | Select appropriate extinguishing agent, enter with BA |
| **Overhaul** | After knockdown, check for re-ignition |

---

## Fire Classes & Extinguishing Agents

| Class | Fire Type | Agents to Use | Do NOT Use |
|-------|-----------|---------------|------------|
| **A** | Solid materials (wood, paper, cloth) | Water, Foam | - |
| **B** | Flammable liquids (oil, fuel) | Foam, CO2, Dry Powder | Water (spreads fire) |
| **C** | Gases (LPG, methane) | Dry Powder, CO2 | Water, Foam |
| **D** | Metals | Special powder | Water (explosive reaction) |
| **E** | Electrical | CO2, Dry Powder | Water (electrocution) |

---

## Fixed Fire Fighting Systems

| System | Location | Activation |
|--------|----------|------------|
| **CO2 Flooding** | Engine Room, Cargo Holds | Manual release from fire control station |
| **Water Spray** | Deck areas, accommodation | Manual activation |
| **Foam** | Cargo areas (some vessels) | Manual activation |

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Before releasing CO2, ensure ALL personnel have evacuated the space. Sound evacuation alarm.

---

## Engine Room Fire Procedure

1. **Activate emergency stop** for affected machinery
2. **Shut ventilation** dampers to engine room
3. **Evacuate all personnel** from engine room
4. **Account for all** engine room crew
5. **Use CO2 flooding** if boundary attack fails
6. **Maintain boundary cooling** for at least 24 hours

## Cargo Hold Fire (Coal)

See detailed procedures in [Cargo Hold Fire - MV Specific](../04-mv-specific/01-cargo-hold-fire.md)

Key differences:
- Coal fires can self-heat and reignite
- CO2 or steam smothering preferred over water
- Water may cause coal dust explosion risk
- Extended monitoring required

| **Lessons learned** | HSSE Manager |
