# Man Overboard (MOB) Procedures

## Purpose

This document provides the procedures for responding to a Man Overboard emergency. Speed of response is critical – survival time in water is limited.

---

## Scope

Applies to: **All vessel types** (FLF, Tug & Barge, Motor Vessels)

---

## Survival Time in Water

| Water Temp | Exhaustion/Unconsciousness | Expected Survival |
|------------|---------------------------|-------------------|
| 0-5°C | 15-30 min | 30-90 min |
| 10-15°C | 30-60 min | 1-2 hours |
| 15-20°C | 2-7 hours | 2-12 hours |
| 20-25°C | 3-12 hours | 3-24+ hours |
| **26-30°C (Indonesia)** | **12+ hours** | **40+ hours** |

> Indonesian waters are warm, but currents, exhaustion, and injuries reduce survival time significantly.

## Immediate Response

### Person Who Witnesses MOB

| Step | Action | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| **1. SHOUT** | "MAN OVERBOARD [PORT/STARBOARD]!" | Alert all personnel |
| **2. POINT** | Point continuously at person in water | Maintain visual contact |
| **3. THROW** | Throw lifebuoy with light/smoke | Mark position, provide flotation |
| **4. INFORM BRIDGE** | "Bridge, man overboard [port/starboard]!" | Start rescue maneuver |
| **5. DON'T JUMP** | Do not enter water unless trained and authorized | Avoid second victim |

### Bridge Response

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **1. Sound Alarm** | 3 prolonged blasts |
| **2. Mark Position** | Press MOB button on GPS, note time and position |
| **3. Maneuver** | Begin Williamson Turn or appropriate recovery maneuver |
| **4. Post Lookouts** | All available eyes on water |
| **5. Prepare Rescue** | Alert rescue boat crew, medical team |
| **6. Notify** | VHF Ch 16 if other vessels can assist |

## Recovery Maneuvers

### Williamson Turn (Standard)
Best for: Immediate response when person seen falling

```
        ← Original course
           │
           │  1. Hard over to same side
           │     as MOB fell
           │
           ↓
         ╭───╮
        ╱     ╲
       │   2.  │ When heading changed 60°
       │       │ shift helm to opposite side
        ╲     ╱
         ╰───╯
           │
           │  3. Steady when reciprocal
           │     course achieved
           ↓
        → Back to MOB position
```

### Anderson Turn (Immediate)
Best for: Very quick initial response

### Scharnow Turn
Best for: Delayed response (person not seen falling)

## Rescue Boat Deployment

### Crew Requirements
- Minimum 3 persons: Coxswain, Bowman, Rescuer
- All wearing lifejackets and equipped with radio

### Deployment Checklist
- [ ] Rescue boat pre-checked
- [ ] Painter secured
- [ ] First aid kit on board
- [ ] Rescue sling/strop ready
- [ ] Radio communication checked
- [ ] Brief received from Bridge

### Recovery Priorities
1. Approach casualty from downwind/down-current
2. Shut down engine near casualty (propeller danger)
3. Use rescue sling to lift – do not pull by arms
4. If conscious: reassure, prevent hypothermia
5. If unconscious: recover horizontally, begin first aid

---

## Medical Response

### On Recovery

| Condition | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **Conscious, uninjured** | Remove wet clothes, blanket, warm drinks, monitor |
| **Conscious, injured** | Treat injuries, keep warm, prepare for MEDEVAC |
| **Unconscious, breathing** | Recovery position, clear airway, keep warm |
| **Unconscious, not breathing** | CPR immediately, prepare AED |

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Hypothermia patients should be handled gently – rough handling can cause cardiac arrest.

---

## Night / Low Visibility MOB

Additional actions:
- Activate all deck lights
- Deploy lifebuoy with light (SOLAS light)
- Use searchlight
- Consider parachute flares to illuminate area
- Request assistance from nearby vessels (VHF Ch 16)
