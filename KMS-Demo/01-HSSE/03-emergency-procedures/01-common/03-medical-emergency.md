# Medical Emergency Procedures

## Purpose

This document provides procedures for responding to medical emergencies on board vessels where immediate hospital access is not available.

---

## Scope

Applies to: **All vessel types** (FLF, Tug & Barge, Motor Vessels)

---

## Medical Emergency Categories

| Category | Examples | Response Level |
|----------|----------|----------------|
| **Critical** | Cardiac arrest, severe bleeding, not breathing | CPR, AED, immediate MEDEVAC |
| **Urgent** | Fractures, burns, chest pain, stroke signs | Stabilize, urgent MEDEVAC |
| **Non-Urgent** | Minor cuts, sprains, seasickness | First aid on board |

---

## Immediate Response

### Person Finding Casualty

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **1. CHECK SCENE** | Ensure area is safe for you and casualty |
| **2. ASSESS** | Check responsiveness: "Are you okay?" + tap shoulder |
| **3. CALL FOR HELP** | Shout for assistance, send for medical kit and AED |
| **4. INFORM BRIDGE** | "Bridge, medical emergency in [Location]" |
| **5. BEGIN FIRST AID** | Start appropriate response based on condition |

---

## Primary Assessment (DRABCD)

| Letter | Check | Action |
|--------|-------|--------|
| **D** | Danger | Is scene safe? Remove hazards |
| **R** | Response | Tap and shout, check for response |
| **A** | Airway | Open airway (head tilt, chin lift) |
| **B** | Breathing | Look, listen, feel for 10 seconds |
| **C** | CPR | If not breathing normally, start CPR |
| **D** | Defibrillation | Apply AED as soon as available |

---

## CPR Procedure (Adult)

### If Not Breathing / No Pulse:

1. **Call for AED**
2. **Position:** Casualty on back, on firm surface
3. **Hand position:** Heel of hand on center of chest
4. **Compressions:** 30 compressions, 5-6 cm deep, 100-120/min
5. **Breaths:** 2 rescue breaths (if trained)
6. **Cycle:** Continue 30:2 until AED arrives or help takes over

> **"Push hard, push fast, don't stop"**

### AED (Automated External Defibrillator)

1. Turn on AED – follow voice prompts
2. Attach pads to bare, dry chest
3. Ensure no one touching casualty during analysis
4. If shock advised, ensure clear and press button
5. Resume CPR immediately after shock
6. Follow AED prompts

## Common Medical Emergencies

### Severe Bleeding

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Apply direct pressure with clean pad |
| 2 | Elevate injured limb if possible |
| 3 | Apply pressure bandage |
| 4 | If blood soaks through, add more pads (don't remove) |
| 5 | Consider tourniquet for life-threatening limb bleeding |

### Suspected Heart Attack

Signs: Chest pain/pressure, shortness of breath, sweating, pain in arm/jaw

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Sit casualty upright, comfortable position |
| 2 | Give aspirin 300mg (if not allergic) |
| 3 | Calm and reassure |
| 4 | Prepare for CPR if condition deteriorates |
| 5 | Arrange urgent MEDEVAC |

### Suspected Stroke

Signs: **F.A.S.T.**
- **F**ace drooping
- **A**rm weakness  
- **S**peech difficulty
- **T**ime to call for help

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Note time symptoms started |
| 2 | Keep casualty calm, upright if conscious |
| 3 | Nothing by mouth |
| 4 | Arrange urgent MEDEVAC |

### Burns

| Severity | Signs | Action |
|----------|-------|--------|
| Minor | Redness, pain | Cool with water 20 min, cover |
| Moderate | Blistering | Cool, don't burst blisters, cover, monitor |
| Severe | Charred, white, deep | Cool carefully, cover loosely, MEDEVAC |

### Fractures

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Immobilize the injured area |
| 2 | Check circulation beyond injury |
| 3 | Apply ice pack (wrapped) |
| 4 | Give pain relief if available |
| 5 | Prepare for transfer to medical facility |

## Medical Evacuation (MEDEVAC)

### MEDEVAC Decision Criteria

MEDEVAC should be considered when:
- Life-threatening condition
- Condition may deteriorate rapidly
- On-board treatment insufficient
- Specialist care required

### MEDEVAC Preparation

1. **Inform DPA and Company** – obtain guidance
2. **Contact MRCC** – if helicopter required
3. **Prepare patient** – stable, strapped to stretcher
4. **Prepare documentation** – medical notes, medications
5. **Prepare deck/helipad** – clear area, fire team standby
