# Oil Spill Response (SOPEP)

## Purpose

This document provides procedures for responding to oil spills in accordance with the **Shipboard Oil Pollution Emergency Plan (SOPEP)** required by MARPOL Annex I.

---

## Scope

Applies to: **All vessel types** (FLF, Tug & Barge, Motor Vessels)

---

## Legal Requirements

- **MARPOL 73/78 Annex I** – Prevention of pollution by oil
- Every vessel ≥400 GT must carry an approved SOPEP
- Spills must be reported to Flag State and nearest coastal state

---

## Oil Spill Response Priority

1. **Safety of personnel** – No response action should endanger crew
2. **Stop the source** – Prevent further spillage
3. **Contain the spread** – Deploy booms, absorbents
4. **Notify authorities** – As required by law
5. **Clean up** – Recover as much oil as possible
6. **Document** – Evidence for investigation

---

## Immediate Response

### Upon Discovering Oil Spill

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **1. ALERT** | Inform Bridge immediately: "Bridge, oil spill [location], [estimated size]" |
| **2. IDENTIFY SOURCE** | Determine where oil is coming from |
| **3. STOP SOURCE** | If safe: close valves, stop pumps, plug leak |
| **4. WARN OTHERS** | Keep personnel away from spill area (slip hazard, vapors) |
| **5. CONTAIN** | Deploy absorbents/booms around deck spills |

### Bridge Response

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **1. Sound Alarm** | Alert crew to oil spill emergency |
| **2. Assess** | Determine if spill is contained or to sea |
| **3. Stop Operations** | Halt bunkering/transfer if in progress |
| **4. Notify Master** | Master takes command of response |
| **5. Prepare Report** | If spill to sea, prepare for notification |

---

## SOPEP Team Duties

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| **Master** | Overall command, decision to report, liaison with authorities |
| **Chief Officer** | On-scene commander, deploy containment team |
| **Chief Engineer** | Identify source, system isolation, machinery issues |
| **Deck Crew** | Deploy booms, absorbents, clean-up |
| **Radio Officer/3/O** | Communications, notifications |

## Containment Equipment (SOPEP Locker)

Every vessel must carry:

| Equipment | Purpose | Quantity |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| Absorbent pads | Small spills on deck | 100 pads |
| Absorbent boom | Contain floating oil | 50 meters |
| Oil boom | Contain larger spills | As fitted |
| Absorbent granules | Deck spills | 2 bags |
| Shovels | Collect contaminated material | 4 |
| Disposal bags | Oily waste collection | 20 |
| Protective equipment | Gloves, goggles, suits | 6 sets |
| Portable pump | Transfer oil | 1 |
| Buckets | Collection | 6 |

## Spill to Sea – Notification Requirements

### When to Report

Spill to sea must be reported if:
- Any discharge of oil or oily mixture
- Probable discharge of oil
- Any discharge during jettisoning for safety

### Notification Content

| Information | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Ship identity | Name, call sign, IMO number |
| Date/time | Of incident |
| Position | Latitude/longitude |
| Type of oil | Fuel oil, lube oil, cargo, etc. |
| Quantity | Estimated amount spilled |
| Sea/weather | Wind, current, sea state |
| Action taken | Response measures initiated |
| Assistance | If required |

### Who to Notify

| Authority | Contact |
|-----------|---------|
| **Nearest Coastal State** | MRCC of nearest coast |
| **Flag State (Indonesia)** | Directorate General of Sea Transportation |
| **Company DPA** | [Company Emergency Number] |
| **Classification Society** | [Class Society Contact] |
| **P&I Club** | [P&I Contact] – for insurance |

## Clean-Up Procedures

### Deck Spills

1. Stop source
2. Surround spill with absorbent boom
3. Apply absorbent pads/granules
4. Collect contaminated material in disposal bags
5. Dispose as oily waste (not overboard)

### Spill to Water

1. Deploy oil boom if alongside (prevent spread to other vessels)
2. Use absorbent boom to collect from water surface
3. Use portable pump to recover oil from boom containment
4. Collect in designated containers for disposal ashore

---

## Documentation

All spills must be documented in:

| Record | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Oil Record Book Part I** | All operational discharges and spills |
| **Deck Log Book** | Time, position, actions taken |
| **SOPEP Incident Log** | Detailed record of response |
| **Photographs** | Evidence of spill and response |
| **Incident Report** | To company within 24 hours |

## Post-Spill Actions

| Action | Responsible | Timeline |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Replenish SOPEP equipment | Chief Officer | Immediately |
| Incident investigation | Master / HSSE | Within 48 hours |
| Report to company | Master | Within 24 hours |
| Corrective actions | As identified | As required |
| Crew debrief | Master | Within 72 hours |
