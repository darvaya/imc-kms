# Barge Collision with FLF

## Purpose

This document provides emergency procedures for when a barge collides with the Floating Loading Facility (FLF) during approach, mooring, or loading operations.

---

## Scope

Applies to: **Floating Loading Facilities (FLF) only**

---

## Collision Risk Factors

| Factor | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Weather** | Strong current, wind, swell affecting barge handling |
| **Communication** | Poor coordination between FLF and tug/barge |
| **Equipment** | Tug engine failure, steering failure |
| **Human** | Misjudgment of distance, fatigue |
| **Structural** | Fendering deterioration |

---

## Immediate Response

### Upon Collision

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **1. SOUND ALARM** | Alert all personnel on FLF |
| **2. STOP OPERATIONS** | Halt cargo transfer immediately |
| **3. ALL HANDS CLEAR** | Move personnel away from impact side |
| **4. ASSESS DAMAGE** | Check for hull breach, structural damage |
| **5. CHECK INJURIES** | Account for all personnel, first aid if needed |
| **6. NOTIFY** | Bridge/Control Room: "FLF, barge collision [location], assessing damage" |

### FLF Control Room Response

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Sound general alarm if significant impact |
| 2 | Stop all cargo operations |
| 3 | Deploy damage control team |
| 4 | Notify Master and DPA |
| 5 | Check stability – any listing or change in draft |
| 6 | Prepare to move barge away if necessary |

---

## Damage Assessment

### Check Immediately:

| Area | What to Check |
|------|---------------|
| **Impact zone** | Dents, fractures, breaches |
| **Waterline** | Any hull penetration, water ingress |
| **Tank soundings** | All ballast/void tanks near impact |
| **Fendering** | Damage to fenders |
| **Mooring equipment** | Bollards, fairleads, lines |
| **Conveyor/crane** | Any structural damage |

### Signs of Serious Damage:

- ⚠️ Water ingress detected
- ⚠️ Visible hull fracture or crack
- ⚠️ Change in list or trim
- ⚠️ Structural deformation
- ⚠️ Fire or sparks at impact point

---

## If Hull Breach / Water Ingress

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Sound emergency alarm |
| 2 | Activate bilge pumps |
| 3 | Attempt to stop/slow ingress (plugs, cement box) |
| 4 | Transfer ballast to correct list if safe |
| 5 | Move barge away from FLF |
| 6 | Prepare for potential evacuation |
| 7 | Request emergency assistance (tug, shore) |

---

## Barge Procedures After Collision

The tug/barge Master should:

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Move barge to safe distance (minimum 50m) |
| 2 | Check own damage (hull, cargo) |
| 3 | Report to FLF duty officer |
| 4 | Await instructions before re-approach |
| 5 | Document incident with photos |

---

## Resumption of Operations

Before resuming cargo operations:

- [ ] Full damage assessment completed
- [ ] No water ingress / stability issues confirmed
- [ ] Structural integrity verified (may require Class survey)
- [ ] Weather conditions acceptable
- [ ] Cause of collision identified and addressed
- [ ] Master/DPA approval obtained
- [ ] Crew briefed on incident

---

## Reporting Requirements

| Report | To Whom | Timeline |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Initial notification | DPA / Company | Immediately |
| Incident report | HSSE Manager | Within 24 hours |
| Class Society | Classification Society | If significant damage |
| Flag State | Indonesia DGST | If significant damage |
| P&I Club | Insurance | Within 24 hours |

---

## Prevention Measures

| Measure | Responsibility |
|---------|----------------|
| Check weather/current before approach | Tug Master |
| Confirm communication channel | FLF & Tug |
| Use experienced helmsman | Tug Master |
| Maintain fendering condition | FLF Chief Officer |
| Speed limit on approach | Tug Master |
| Pilot/adviser if high risk | Operations |
