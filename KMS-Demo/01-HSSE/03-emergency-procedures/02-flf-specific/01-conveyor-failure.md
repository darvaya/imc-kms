# Conveyor System Failure (FLF)

## Purpose

This document provides emergency procedures for conveyor system failures on Floating Loading Facilities (FLF), which are critical to transshipment operations.

---

## Scope

Applies to: **Floating Loading Facilities (FLF) only**

---

## Conveyor System Overview

FLF conveyors transfer coal from barges to mother vessels at high speed. Failures can result in:
- Personnel injury (entanglement, struck by)
- Coal spillage (environmental, slip hazard)
- Cargo damage or loss
- Operational delays

---

## Types of Conveyor Failures

| Failure Type | Signs | Risk Level |
|--------------|-------|------------|
| **Belt Misalignment** | Belt tracking off-center, scraping | Medium |
| **Belt Tear/Break** | Visible damage, coal spillage | High |
| **Drive Motor Failure** | Belt stops, burning smell | Medium |
| **Roller Failure** | Unusual noise, friction, sparks | High |
| **Control System Failure** | Belt won't stop or start | Critical |
| **Fire in Conveyor** | Smoke, flames from friction | Critical |

---

## Immediate Response

### Upon Detecting Conveyor Problem

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **1. EMERGENCY STOP** | Press nearest emergency stop button immediately |
| **2. STAND CLEAR** | Move away from conveyor – do not attempt to grab loose material |
| **3. ALERT** | Notify Control Room: "Control, conveyor [number] emergency stop, [problem]" |
| **4. ISOLATE** | If safe, ensure electrical isolation |
| **5. ASSESS** | Visual inspection from safe distance |

### Control Room Response

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Confirm conveyor stopped, electrical isolation in place |
| 2 | Notify Master and Chief Engineer |
| 3 | Stop cargo operations until assessed |
| 4 | Notify attending barge/vessel |
| 5 | Dispatch maintenance team |

---

## Emergency Stop Locations

| Location | Color | Activation |
|----------|-------|------------|
| Along conveyor length | Red mushroom | Push to stop |
| Control Room | Red button | Push to stop |
| Pull-cord | Red cord | Pull anywhere along belt |

> **IMPORTANT:** All personnel must know the location of nearest emergency stops.

---

## Belt Fire Procedures

If fire is detected on conveyor:

1. **Activate emergency stop** – belt must stop immediately
2. **Sound fire alarm** – notify all personnel
3. **Do not use water initially** – may spread coal fire
4. **Deploy CO2/dry powder** extinguishers
5. **Isolate electrical supply**
6. **Boundary cooling** – prevent fire spread to structures
7. **Do not restart** until fully inspected and cleared

---

## Personnel Entanglement

If someone is caught in conveyor:

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **1. EMERGENCY STOP** | Immediate – do not try to reverse belt |
| **2. CALL FOR HELP** | Medical emergency, notify bridge |
| **3. DO NOT MOVE** | Do not move casualty unless further danger |
| **4. ISOLATE POWER** | Lock out/tag out before any rescue attempt |
| **5. FIRST AID** | Treat injuries, prepare for MEDEVAC |
| **6. PRESERVE SCENE** | For investigation |

---

## Belt Break / Coal Spillage

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Stop all cargo operations |
| 2 | Assess spillage – deck or to sea |
| 3 | If to sea, initiate oil spill-like response for coal |
| 4 | Deploy crew to clear spillage from deck |
| 5 | Prevent slip/fall hazards |
| 6 | Document with photographs |
| 7 | Repair or replace belt section |

---

## Restart After Emergency Stop

Before restarting conveyor:

- [ ] Cause of emergency identified and rectified
- [ ] Belt visually inspected full length
- [ ] Guards and covers in place
- [ ] All personnel clear of conveyor
- [ ] Warning siren sounded (3 times)
- [ ] Slow start-up, observe for issues
- [ ] Master/Chief Engineer approval obtained
