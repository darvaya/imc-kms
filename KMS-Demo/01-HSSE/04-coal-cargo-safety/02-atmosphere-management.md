# Cargo Hold Atmosphere Management

## Purpose

This document provides procedures for monitoring and managing the atmosphere in cargo holds containing coal to prevent explosion, asphyxiation, and fire hazards.

---

## Scope

Applies to: **Motor Vessels (Bulk Carriers) carrying coal cargo**

---

## Gases of Concern

| Gas | Symbol | Hazard | Action Level |
|-----|--------|--------|--------------|
| **Oxygen** | O2 | Depletion (asphyxiation) | <19.5% critical |
| **Methane** | CH4 | Explosion | >1% (20% LEL) |
| **Carbon Monoxide** | CO | Toxicity, fire indicator | >25 ppm |
| **Carbon Dioxide** | CO2 | Asphyxiation | >0.5% |
| **Hydrogen** | H2 | Explosion, fire indicator | >1% |

---

## Monitoring Equipment

### Required Instruments:

| Instrument | Purpose | Calibration |
|------------|---------|-------------|
| Multi-gas detector | O2, CH4, CO, H2S | Per manufacturer (usually monthly) |
| Oxygen analyzer | Accurate O2 reading | Monthly |
| Explosive meter | LEL measurement | Monthly |
| Temperature sensor | Cargo temperature | Before each voyage |

### Sampling Points:

- Sampling pipes fitted to each hold
- Sample from **top** of cargo space (gases rise/accumulate differently)
- Multiple points if hold is partitioned

---

## Monitoring Frequency

| Phase | Frequency |
|-------|-----------|
| **Loading** | Every 4 hours |
| **Sea passage (<10 days)** | Every 8 hours |
| **Sea passage (>10 days)** | Every 12 hours |
| **If abnormal reading** | Every 4 hours or continuous |
| **Before hold entry** | Immediately before |

---

## Monitoring Record

For each reading, record:

| Data Point | Example |
|------------|---------|
| Date/Time | 02-Jan-2026 0800 |
| Hold Number | #2 |
| O2 % | 20.1% |
| CH4 % | 0.3% |
| CO ppm | 5 ppm |
| Cargo Temp (°C) | 38°C |
| Weather | Clear, 29°C |
| Signature | Chief Officer |

---

## Ventilation Strategy

### Two Approaches:

| Strategy | Method | When to Use |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| **Surface Ventilation** | Keep hatches closed, limited natural vent | Normal conditions |
| **Forced Ventilation** | Mechanical supply of fresh air | NOT for coal |
| **Sealing** | Close all vents, hatches, openings | If CH4 or CO elevated |

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Forced ventilation supplies oxygen that accelerates self-heating. Use with extreme caution.

### Decision Matrix:

| Condition | Ventilation Action |
|-----------|--------------------|
| All readings normal | Surface ventilation only |
| CH4 > 1% | **CLOSE ventilation immediately** |
| CO > 30 ppm | **CLOSE ventilation, monitor temp** |
| Cargo temp > 55°C | **CLOSE ventilation, seek advice** |
| O2 < 10% and stable | Maintain sealed (fire suppression) |

---

## Abnormal Readings – Response

### Elevated Methane (CH4 > 1%)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Stop all cargo operations |
| 2 | Close all ventilators and openings |
| 3 | Notify Master immediately |
| 4 | Increase monitoring frequency (every 4 hrs) |
| 5 | Inform company / DPA |
| 6 | Consider route to port of refuge |

### Elevated Carbon Monoxide (CO > 30 ppm)

CO elevation indicates heating/oxidation in progress.

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | CLOSE all ventilation to hold |
| 2 | NO ENTRY to hold |
| 3 | Monitor cargo temperature |
| 4 | Notify Master and company |
| 5 | Prepare for cargo fire scenario |
| 6 | Seek expert advice (Class, P&I) |

### Low Oxygen (O2 < 19.5%)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | NO ENTRY to hold (IDLH atmosphere) |
| 2 | If occurs rapidly, suggests active oxidation |
| 3 | Continue monitoring |
| 4 | If natural decline, may indicate coal consuming O2 (normal for some coals) |

---

## Hold Entry Requirements

**NO ENTRY** to cargo holds unless:

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Atmosphere tested | O2: 19.5%-23%, CH4: <1%, CO: <25 ppm |
| Permit issued | Confined Space Entry Permit |
| Ventilation | Continuous fresh air supply |
| Standby person | At entry point |
| Communication | Radio or voice |
| Rescue equipment | Ready for immediate use |
| PPE | Coveralls, hard hat, boots, BA available |
