# Coal Properties & Hazards

## Purpose

This document provides essential knowledge about coal cargo properties and associated hazards that all personnel involved in coal handling must understand.

---

## Scope

Applies to: **All vessels carrying coal cargo (MV, Barges, FLF)**

---

## Types of Coal

| Type | Carbon % | Volatility | Self-Heating Risk |
|------|----------|------------|-------------------|
| **Anthracite** | 86-98% | Low | Low |
| **Bituminous** | 45-86% | Medium | Medium |
| **Sub-bituminous** | 35-45% | High | High |
| **Lignite (Brown Coal)** | 25-35% | Very High | Very High |

> Most Indonesian thermal coal is **sub-bituminous** with HIGH self-heating potential.

---

## Self-Heating Process

Coal self-heating occurs through oxidation – coal reacts with oxygen and releases heat.

### Factors Increasing Self-Heating Risk:

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Small particle size** | More surface area for oxidation |
| **High moisture** | Accelerates reaction then dries out |
| **Free access to air** | Provides oxygen for reaction |
| **Previous wetting** | Coal that was wet, then dried, is more reactive |
| **High inherent sulfur** | Sulfur compounds are more reactive |
| **Freshly mined coal** | More reactive than stockpiled |

### Self-Heating Stages:

```
Stage 1: Initial Heating (Ambient → 40°C)
        ↓
Stage 2: Accelerated Heating (40°C → 80°C)
        ↓
Stage 3: Self-Sustaining (80°C → 150°C)
        ↓
Stage 4: Ignition (>150°C) 🔥
```

## Methane Emission

Coal releases **methane (CH4)** – a flammable gas.

| Risk | Level |
|------|-------|
| Flammability | LEL 5% - UEL 15% in air |
| Explosive | When mixed with air in enclosed space |
| Source | Trapped in coal pores, released during handling |

### Methane Monitoring:
- Measure CH4 in cargo hold atmosphere daily
- **Action Level:** CH4 > 20% LEL (1% by volume)
- If exceeded: Seal hold, stop ventilation, monitor

---

## Oxygen Depletion

Coal **absorbs oxygen** from the atmosphere – this creates dual hazards:

| Hazard | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Asphyxiation** | Reduced O2 in hold can kill without warning |
| **Supports self-heating** | Oxygen feeds the oxidation process |

### Safe Oxygen Levels:
- Normal atmosphere: 21% O2
- **Minimum safe for entry:** 19.5% O2
- **Danger:** Below 19.5% O2 – do not enter

---

## Coal Dust Hazards

| Hazard | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Explosion** | Coal dust in air is explosive (like flour) |
| **Respiratory** | Inhalation causes lung disease (Black Lung) |
| **Slip hazard** | Accumulation on deck is slippery when wet |
| **Visibility** | Heavy dust reduces visibility during loading |

### Dust Control Measures:
- Water spray during loading/discharge
- Avoid wind-facing loading when possible
- PPE: Dust masks (P2/N95 minimum)
- Regular deck washing

---

## Cargo Shift

Improperly trimmed coal can shift when vessel rolls.

| Cause | Prevention |
|-------|------------|
| Untrimmed peaks | Level cargo surface after loading |
| Liquefaction | Check Transportable Moisture Limit (TML) |
| Heavy weather | Adjust voyage to avoid excessive rolling |

> **IMO Requirement:** Coal must be trimmed to the boundaries of the cargo hold.

---

## Cargo Declaration Requirements

Before loading, the shipper must provide:

| Information | Why Required |
|-------------|--------------|
| Coal type and grade | Assess self-heating risk |
| Size distribution | Fines increase dust and heating risk |
| Moisture content | Must be below TML |
| Sulfur content | High sulfur = higher risk |
| History of self-heating | Known problem cargo |
| Expected behavior | Special handling requirements |

> **Master's Right:** Master may refuse cargo if declaration is inadequate.

---

## Personnel Awareness Summary

### All Crew Must Know:

1. ⚠️ **Never enter a coal hold without atmosphere testing and permit**
2. 🔥 **Coal can catch fire without a spark – from self-heating**
3. 💨 **Coal dust can explode – no ignition sources during cargo ops**
4. 😮‍💨 **Coal absorbs oxygen – holds may be oxygen-deficient**
5. 📊 **Report any unusual heat, smoke, or smell immediately**
