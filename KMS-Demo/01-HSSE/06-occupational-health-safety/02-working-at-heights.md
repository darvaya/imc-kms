# Working at Heights

## Purpose

This document provides safety procedures for all work at heights on IMC Pelita Logistik vessels and facilities.

---

## Scope

Applies to: **All personnel on all vessels and facilities**

---

## Definition

**Work at heights** is any work where a person could fall a distance capable of causing personal injury (generally **2 meters or more**).

Examples:
- Work on masts, funnels, cranes
- Painting over ship's side
- Cargo hold inspections (from ladders/staging)
- Staging/scaffold work
- Gangway operations

---

## Hierarchy of Controls

| Priority | Control | Examples |
|----------|---------|----------|
| 1 | **Avoid** work at heights | Do task from ground level |
| 2 | **Prevent** falls | Guard rails, barriers, staging |
| 3 | **Mitigate** falls | Fall arrest systems (harness) |
| 4 | **Minimize** consequences | Rescue planning |

## Fall Protection Equipment

### Full Body Harness

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Fit | Adjusted to body, all straps secured |
| Attachment point | Dorsal D-ring primary |
| Inspection | Before each use |
| Standards | EN 361, ANSI Z359 |

### Lanyards

| Type | Use |
|------|-----|
| Shock-absorbing lanyard | Primary fall arrest |
| Double lanyard (twin-tail) | 100% tie-off when moving |
| Positioning lanyard | Work positioning only (not fall arrest) |

### Anchor Points

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Strength | Minimum 22 kN (2,200 kg) |
| Location | Above work position where possible |
| Fixed | Engineered anchor or certified temporary |
| Independence | Do not share anchors |

---

## Procedures

### Always:

- ✅ Maintain 100% tie-off (always attached)
- ✅ Use two attachment points when moving
- ✅ Keep lanyard short to reduce fall distance
- ✅ Attach at or above shoulder level
- ✅ Secure tools with lanyards (prevent drops)
- ✅ Work from stable platform when possible

### Never:

- ❌ Work at heights alone
- ❌ Use damaged equipment
- ❌ Exceed anchor point capacity
- ❌ Work in unsuitable weather
- ❌ Remove guardrails without fall protection
- ❌ Overreach from ladders/platforms

## Ladder Safety

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Angle | 4:1 ratio (1m out for every 4m up) |
| Securing | Tied at top or secured at base |
| Extension | Extends 1m above landing |
| Condition | No damage, rungs secure |
| Three-point contact | When ascending/descending |
| Facing ladder | Never face outward |

---

## Scaffolding

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Erection | By competent person |
| Inspection | Before first use, after modification |
| Loading | Not exceeded |
| Guardrails | Full protection on elevated platforms |
| Tag system | Green (safe), Red (do not use) |

## Work Over Ship's Side

Additional requirements when working over water:

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Permit | Work over side permit required |
| Lifejacket | Worn by worker |
| Supervision | Continuous visual |
| Rescue | Rescue boat ready, crew on standby |
| Shark watch | In shark-prone areas |

---

## Rescue Planning

Before any work at heights, ensure:

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Rescue method | Identified and practiced |
| Rescue equipment | Ready for use (rescue kit, descender) |
| Trained rescuer | On standby |
| Time limit | Suspension trauma – rescue within 15 min |
| First aid | Medical team informed |

### Suspension Trauma

A person suspended in a harness after a fall can suffer serious injury if not rescued quickly:
- Blood pooling in legs
- Cardiovascular strain
- **Rescue within 15 minutes is critical**

---

## Weather Limits

| Condition | Limit |
|-----------|-------|
| Wind | Suspend if > 25 knots |
| Rain | Suspend if surfaces slippery |
| Lightning | Suspend immediately |
| Vessel motion | Suspend if excessive rolling/pitching |
