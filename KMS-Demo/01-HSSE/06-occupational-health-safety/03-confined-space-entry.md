# Confined Space Entry

## Purpose

This document provides safety procedures for entry into confined spaces on IMC Pelita Logistik vessels and facilities.

---

## Scope

Applies to: **All personnel on all vessels and facilities**

---

## Definition

A **confined space** is an enclosed or partially enclosed space that:
- Is not designed for continuous human occupancy
- Has restricted entry/exit
- May have a hazardous atmosphere or other hazards

### Examples on Vessels:
- Cargo holds
- Ballast tanks
- Fuel tanks
- Void spaces
- Pump rooms
- Duct keels
- Chain lockers

---

## Confined Space Hazards

| Hazard | Risk |
|--------|------|
| **Oxygen deficiency** | Suffocation (<19.5% O2) |
| **Oxygen enrichment** | Fire/explosion (>23% O2) |
| **Toxic gases** | H2S, CO, cargo vapors |
| **Flammable atmosphere** | Explosion (>LEL) |
| **Engulfment** | Drowning in liquid or solid material |
| **Physical hazards** | Machinery, falls, restricted movement |
| **Heat stress** | High temperature |

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Confined space accidents often result in multiple fatalities when rescuers enter without protection.

---

## Permit Requirements

**ALL confined space entries require a Confined Space Entry Permit.**

No exceptions. Even for "quick checks."

---

## Entry Procedure

### Pre-Entry Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Permit issued** | Signed by Master/Chief Officer |
| **Risk assessment** | JSA completed |
| **Atmosphere tested** | Immediately before entry |
| **Ventilation** | Minimum 10 air changes before entry |
| **Communication** | Established with standby |
| **Standby person** | At entry point |
| **Rescue equipment** | Ready for immediate use |
| **BA/SCBA** | Available if atmosphere uncertain |

### Atmosphere Testing

| Gas | Safe Range | Danger Level |
|-----|------------|--------------|
| **Oxygen (O2)** | 19.5% - 23% | <19.5% or >23% |
| **Flammable gases (LEL)** | <1% LEL | >1% LEL |
| **Carbon monoxide (CO)** | <25 ppm | >25 ppm |
| **Hydrogen sulfide (H2S)** | <10 ppm | >10 ppm |

> **Test at multiple levels** – gases have different densities.

### Testing Protocol

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Calibrate gas detector before use |
| 2 | Test from outside the space |
| 3 | Lower detector on rope if needed |
| 4 | Test top, middle, and bottom |
| 5 | Record readings on permit |
| 6 | Continuous monitoring during work |

---

## During Entry

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Continuous ventilation** | Must not stop during work |
| **Continuous monitoring** | Gas detector running |
| **Communication** | Regular check-ins with standby |
| **Standby person** | Never leaves post |
| **Entry log** | Names, times in/out recorded |
| **Escape plan** | Known to all entrants |

### Standby Person Responsibilities

- Remain at entry point at all times
- Maintain communication with entrants
- Count personnel in/out
- Initiate alarm if emergency
- **DO NOT ENTER** to rescue – call for help

---

## Emergency Procedures

### If Atmosphere Alarms:

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | EVACUATE immediately |
| 2 | Account for all personnel |
| 3 | Notify bridge |
| 4 | Investigate before re-entry |

### If Person Unconscious Inside:

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Sound alarm – DO NOT ENTER without BA |
| 2 | Call for rescue team |
| 3 | If trained and equipped, use rescue harness to extract |
| 4 | Begin first aid once out of space |
| 5 | Request medical evacuation if needed |

> ⚠️ **CRITICAL:** Never enter to rescue without proper equipment. Many deaths occur to would-be rescuers.

---

## Rescue Equipment

The following must be **ready for immediate use** at the entry point:

| Equipment | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| Rescue harness (tripod/davit if fitted) | Extract casualty |
| Self-contained breathing apparatus (SCBA) | Rescuer protection |
| Resuscitator | First aid |
| First aid kit | Treat casualties |
| Radio | Communication |
| Torch/flashlight | Visibility |

---

## Personal Protective Equipment

| PPE | Required |
|-----|----------|
| Hard hat | Yes |
| Safety boots | Yes |
| Coveralls | Yes |
| Gloves | Yes |
| Rescue harness/lifeline | Yes |
| Gas detector (personal) | Recommended |
| BA/SCBA | If atmosphere suspect |

---

## Permit Duration

| Standard | Details |
|----------|---------|
| Maximum 8 hours | Or one work shift |
| Re-assessment required | If work scope changes |
| New permit required | For next entry |
| Suspend permit | If conditions change |

---

## Training

All personnel who enter confined spaces must have:

| Training | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|
| Confined space awareness | Initial + 3 years |
| Gas detector use | Annual |
| Rescue procedures | Annual drill |
